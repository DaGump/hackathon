# Hackathon Challenge  
## Ruin the UX. Keep the player alive.

Welcome to probably the worst good idea of this hackathon.

You’ll receive a fully accessible, well-built, and usable video player.  
Your mission: turn it into a frustrating, confusing, slightly painful experience — without breaking it.

---

## ⏱️ Setup

- **Time limit:** 60 minutes  
- **Mode:** Teamwork  
- **Tech stack:** HTML, CSS, JavaScript  
- **Rule #1:** The video must still play  
- **Rule #2:** Do not break the app completely  

**Unofficial motto:**  
> Don’t break the app. Break the spirit.

---

## 🎯 Mission

The goal is *not* to destroy the page.

The goal is to take something that works well and make it:
- confusing  
- annoying  
- unnecessarily complex  
- subtly wrong  

The player must remain functional — everything around it is fair game.

---

## 💡 The Feeling You’re Aiming For

- “Why is it like this?”  
- “It works… but it feels terrible.”  
- “Who thought this was a good idea?”  

---

## ⚙️ Rules

- Only frontend changes (HTML / CSS / JS)
- The video player must remain usable
- No blank pages
- No hard crashes
- No complete feature removal

---

## 💥 What’s Encouraged

### 1. Confusing interactions  
Things that *look* logical but aren’t.

### 2. Unpredictable behavior  
Almost correct is worse than completely wrong.

### 3. Subtle UX sabotage  
The more hidden the frustration, the better.

### 4. Questionable UI decisions  
Technically allowed. Practically terrible.

---

## 🚫 What Doesn’t Count

- Deleting everything  
- Breaking the app completely  
- Random chaos without a concept  

Crashes are boring. Functional frustration is the goal.

---

## 🏆 Judging Criteria

You don’t win by causing the most damage.  
You win by creating the most *elegant digital cruelty*.

### Creativity  
Original ideas over obvious hacks.

### Subtlety  
Best solutions feel almost normal.

### Frustration Level  
How bad does it feel to use?

### Hackathon Spirit  
Creative, technical, slightly evil.

---

## 🎤 Final Presentation

Each team presents:

- **A:** What did you change?  
- **B:** Why is it worse now?  
- **C:** Which UX / accessibility principles did you ignore (on purpose)?  

---

## 🧠 Final Thought

Build something that works perfectly —  
and still makes people want to leave.
